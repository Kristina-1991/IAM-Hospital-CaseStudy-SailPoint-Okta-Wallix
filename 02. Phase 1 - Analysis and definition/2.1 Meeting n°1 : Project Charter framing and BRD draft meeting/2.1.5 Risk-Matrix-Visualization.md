## Legend and Assessment assumptions 
### Impact levels (vertical axis) 
Impact is evaluated qualitatively based on the potential consequences of the risk on clinical operations, patient safety, regulatory compliance, and data confidentiality.

| Level            | Definition                                                                                                 |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1 â€“ Very Low** | Minor disruption, no patient impact, no sensitive data exposure.                                           |
| **2 â€“ Low**      | Limited operational disruption, minimal data exposure.                                                     |
| **3 â€“ Medium**   | Noticeable operational impact, exposure of non-critical health data, minor compliance deviation.           |
| **4 â€“ High**     | Significant disruption, potential impact on patient care, exposure of sensitive data, GDPR non-compliance. |
| **5 â€“ Critical** | Severe patient safety risk, major operational breakdown, large-scale data breach, legal consequences.      |

### Probability levels (horizontal axis)
Probability reflects the likelihood of occurrence based on current (AS-IS) controls, workflow maturity, and historical incidents in healthcare environments.

| Level                 | Definition                                                      |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1 â€“ Very unlikely** | Rare occurrence, strong existing controls.                      |
| **2 â€“ Unlikely**      | Could occur but not expected under normal conditions.           |
| **3 â€“ Possible**      | Occurs occasionally; process weaknesses exist.                  |
| **4 â€“ Likely**        | Has occurred or is expected to occur frequently.                |
| **5 â€“ Very likely**   | High recurrence rate; lack of controls makes it almost certain. |

### Risk level calculation 
Risk level = **Impact x Probability**

## Glossary of key terms 

**ABAC (Attribute-Based Access Control)** : Access based on user attributes (role, department, location, device).

**RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)** : Access based on predefined roles aligned with job functions.

**PBAC (Policy-Based Access Control)** : Dynamic access decisions based on contextual rules and policies.

**IGA (Identity Governance & Administration)** :IAM capability managing identity lifecycle, roles, certification, SoD.

**IDP (Identity Provider)** : Central platform for authentication, SSO, MFA (e.g., Okta).

**PAM (Privileged Access Management)** : Controls and monitors privileged accounts (e.g., Wallix).

**Therapeutic Link** : Official verification ensuring a doctor has a legitimate relation with a patient before accessing or prescribing.

**SoD (Separation of Duties)** : Ensuring critical actions cannot be performed by one person without validation.

**Likelihood / Probability** : Estimated chance that a risk materializes.

**Impact** : Consequences of a risk on operations, patient safety, compliance.

**HL7**: Standard for exchanging medical data between hospital systems.

**Audit Trail** : Trace of all user actions for accountability.

**Masquerading (ISO 27799)**: When an attacker impersonates a legitimate user or system.
